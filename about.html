About the publication

A small paper, about a big game.

We started The Half Space because we wanted somewhere to read about football the way we used to read about everything else. Slowly. With pictures. With a pencil in our hand.

The half space is a tactical term — the strip of pitch between the wing and the central channel, where modern playmakers prefer to live. It is not a place you go on the way to somewhere else. It is the destination. That is the spirit of the writing here.

We are a publication for readers who like the diagrams and the long sentences in equal measure. We cover the major leagues, the European nights, and the games nobody else is writing about, with the same care.

No takes. No tribalism. No churn. One Saturday letter in the inbox, a handful of dispatches in the week, and the occasional long read when the subject earns it.

The editorial credo
i.

Slowness

We publish when a piece is ready, not when the news cycle says we should. Some essays are written in an afternoon. Some take a season. Both are welcome.

ii.

Care

Every piece is read by at least two editors before it reaches you. We fact-check tactical claims the way other publications fact-check politics. The diagrams are drawn by hand.

iii.

Reading

We assume our readers know the game. We don't talk down. We try to teach where teaching is needed, and to trust where trust is earned.
