About the publication

A small paper, about a big game.

We started The Half Space because we wanted somewhere to read about football the way we used to read about everything else. Slowly. With a notebook. With a pencil in our hand.

The half space is borrowed from the language of the pitch — but it is meant here as a kind of editorial position. The bit between the news and the analysis. Between the match and the long view. Between what was said in the tunnel and what ended up in the morning's papers.

We write match reports, comment, and features. We try to file the morning after, not the moment the whistle goes. We are interested in the games other writing is too quick to leave behind.

No takes. No tribalism. No churn. One Saturday letter in the inbox, a handful of dispatches in the week, and the occasional long read when the subject earns it.

The editorial credo
i.

Slowness

We publish when a piece is ready, not when the news cycle says we should. Some essays are written in an afternoon. Some take a season. Both are welcome.

ii.

Care

Every piece is read by at least two editors before it reaches you. We attend the matches we write about wherever we can. We get the names right, the scorelines right, and the smaller facts right.

iii.

Reading

We assume our readers know the game. We don't talk down. We try to teach where teaching is needed, and to trust where trust is earned.
