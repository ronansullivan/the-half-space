Hi, and welcome to our live blog for today's final gameweek of this Premier League season.

We're going to be following Spurs against Everton at the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium, as they require a draw to avoid relegation and send West Ham down, who themselves are facing Leeds at the London Stadium.

Spurs are currently 17th and on 38 points, while West Ham are 18th on 36 points. If Spurs lose and West Ham win, Spurs will go down. All other combinations of results will see the north London side stay up, in what has been such a poor season from them.

3:30pm

How did Spurs get here?

A lot of terrible decisions…

3:31pm

Frank-ly awful

Spurs have been — to put it mildly — terrible this season.

In firing Ange Postecoglou 16 days after their Europa League triumph over Manchester United in Bilbao, Spurs hired Brentford manager Thomas Frank, who had guided the West London side to their joint-best league finish of ninth.

Frank was seen as a steadier hand than the high-wire act of Postecoglou, who was often criticised for prioritising attacking football over defensive solidity.

However, despite a positive start — coming within seconds of winning the European Super Cup against PSG in his first game, followed by a clinical win away at Manchester City — the wheels started to fall off.

Fans turned on the Dane as Spurs' attack faltered, with record-low xG totals against Chelsea (0.05) and Arsenal (0.07) in November — and he could never really win them back.

Results never recovered, with a 2-0 home win against Brentford in December the last time Spurs would win at home under Frank, who was eventually sacked in February after a run of two wins in 17 games.

3:32pm

Tudor is to do?

Sporting directors Vinai Venkatesham and Johan Lange turned to former Croatia international and Juventus manager Igor Tudor to bail them out of trouble.

With experience of being parachuted into Italian clubs late in the season, Venkatesham and Lange took former consultant Fabio Paratici's recommendation and hired the gruff Croat.

It didn't work out.

Tudor was fired after just 44 days in charge, his last game being a crucial match against relegation rivals Nottingham Forest at the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium. Spurs fans lined the streets ahead of kick-off, cheering on the team coach as they arrived. There was hope in the air.

Spurs lost 3-0.

Tudor was informed of his father's passing immediately after the game, so did not fulfil his media duties, with Spurs confirming his exit a week after the game, bringing in…

3:33pm

Save us, Roberto! Save us!

After leaving Marseille on the same day Frank was sacked by Spurs, many were surprised when the Lilywhites announced the Italian had joined on a five-year contract, who seemed to have turned down Spurs in the wake of Frank's departure.

De Zerbi's first game against Sunderland away resulted in a tight 1-0 loss, but there were signs of improvement from Frank and Tudor's ill-fated reigns.

What followed was a heart-breaking 2-2 home draw with Brighton, where Spurs conceded a 96th-minute equaliser. Many assumed Spurs were doomed from that point, with West Ham's results having put them into the relegation zone the week prior.

Spurs faced Wolves away next, having to secure a win — and they did! But with West Ham securing a late win over Everton they were still 18th.

The tide turned however, when West Ham were walloped by Brentford 3-0, and Spurs put on a masterclass at Aston Villa, winning 2-1 and moving into 17th.

Now, after Spurs drew with Leeds and lost to Chelsea, and West Ham lost to Arsenal and Newcastle, the relegation scrap has come down to the final day.

Can de Zerbi do what many thought impossible after Tudor's last game? Can he keep Spurs up?

We'll find out in just under half an hour at the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium, as the home side take on Everton in this huge game.
